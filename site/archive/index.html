the thesis
agents need to transact onchain. the tooling is fragmented, unaudited, unsafe.
step 01
the problem
agents need to transact onchain but tooling is fragmented across dozens of libraries. nothing is audited, nothing is safe to run autonomously.
step 02
the toolkit
MLTL gives agents audited, one-command onchain actions. launch tokens, trade markets, pay for APIs, manage wallets — all from a single CLI.
step 03
the market
humans interact with agent activity through tokens, trades, and price signals. the market becomes the interface between machine action and human attention.
MLTL is the economic layer for agents — every onchain action, one audited command.
